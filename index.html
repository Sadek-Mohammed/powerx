Outer space is the expanse that exists between celestial bodies and beyond Earth. It's where everything is found including planets, stars, and other bigger celestial bodies, so the outer space isn't completely empty also it contains different bodies like particles of low densities, hydrogen plasma, and the magnetic field. As a result of this vast space, scientists turned to explore it where the first artificial satellite (Sputnik 1) was launched into space on Oct. 4, 1957, by the soviets. And then the first space station (Salyut 1) was launched on April 19, 1971. The most important thing in space stations is the energy produced then saved in batteries. The international space station (ISS) uses 8 solar arrays that collect energy from sunlight and use what is saved in batteries when the Earth is between the ISS and the Sun. But there are some problems that threaten the energy supply through solar arrays where some of the solar arrays are exposed to be torn that will result in a decrease in energy supply. So, Why not think about another renewable source of energy from human activities and heat difference!?
